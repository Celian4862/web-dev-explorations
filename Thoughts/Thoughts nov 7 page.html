So, I was listening to this audio about the 80/20 rule, which is basically about putting in twenty percent of your efforts to get eighty percent of your results. Later in the audio, I heard the narrator say that you shouldn't be good at many things, but you should instead just focus on one thing to master. Previously, I thought that it was better to be good at many things in order to be self-sufficient. This made me question: "Is it truly better to be an expert at one thing or be decent in many things?

And so my research began. Though the internet isn't particularly helpful due to its abundance of supposedly relevant but actually irrelevant results, I managed to find a few articles that talk about this issue of mine. From what I understand from these articles, both mastering one skill and being decent at many are equally important. Here's why.

  • Innovation and creativity require knowledge of more than one field. you need to combine different things to create new things.
  • Although it's not bad to master one skill, it's beneficial to know a bit of other skills, such as cooking. If you have to go and eat out every time, you may not be able to choose what you'll eat.
  • You could be well-known for your skills if you are extraordinary in such a skill. But if you are good in only that skill and not at least decent in some basic ones, people may no longer find you so impressive.
  • You can also try moving on to master another skill if you feel confident in your first skill. Don't forget to continually practice your skills.
